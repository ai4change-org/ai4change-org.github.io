Our First 5 Problems

March 28, 2026 AI4Change Team 6 min read
Share Copied!

Every movement needs a first step. For AI4Change, that step is choosing which problems to tackle first. Not the easiest problems. Not the most technically interesting. The ones where real people are hurting the most — and where AI can make the biggest difference the fastest.

Today we are announcing our five seed problems: the first entries on the AI4Change Problem Board. These are the challenges that will attract our first champions, our first teams, and our first shipped solutions.

How We Chose

We didn't sit in a conference room with whiteboards. We asked. We listened. We looked at where the gap between "problem" and "solution" was widest, and where AI could bridge it.

We filtered for three things: human urgency (are people suffering right now?), AI leverage (can AI dramatically accelerate the solution?), and open-source viability (can the solution be built in the open, by a distributed community, and given away for free?). Every problem that made this list scored high on all three.

We don't pick problems because they're interesting to technologists. We pick them because they're unbearable to the people living them.

The Five Problems

💧
Environment

Water Quality Monitoring

2 billion people drink contaminated water daily. They know the water might be bad. They drink it anyway, because there is no alternative and no warning system.

AI-powered sensors combined with satellite imagery can detect contamination in real time and alert communities before people get sick. The technology exists. The data exists. What's missing is the open-source pipeline that puts it all together — an end-to-end system that any community, anywhere, can deploy without a PhD or a corporate contract.

🌾
Agriculture

Crop Disease Detection

Smallholder farmers lose up to 40% of their harvest to diseases they can't identify until it's too late. By the time the leaves turn yellow or the fruit rots, the damage is done. Entire seasons. Entire livelihoods. Gone.

A phone camera and an AI model can identify crop diseases instantly and suggest treatment — the same way a dermatologist app identifies a skin condition. This single capability could save millions of families from food insecurity. The models exist in research papers. We need to put them in farmers' hands.

🏥
Health

Medical Supply Tracking

In remote regions, medical supplies run out because nobody predicted the demand spike. A clinic in rural Malawi runs out of malaria medication during rainy season — every single year. Not because the medication doesn't exist, but because the supply chain can't see what's coming.

AI logistics can forecast need based on seasonal patterns, population data, and historical demand. It can optimize delivery routes, predict stockouts before they happen, and ensure vaccines and medications reach people when they need them — not weeks later.

📚
Education

Education Content Generation

250 million children lack access to quality learning materials in their own language. They sit in classrooms with outdated textbooks written for a different culture, a different context, a different reality. Or they have no textbooks at all.

AI can generate, translate, and culturally adapt educational content for any language and any context — and it can do it at scale, for free. A math lesson for a 10-year-old in Quechua. A science module for a teenager in Amharic. Locally relevant, pedagogically sound, and infinitely reproducible.

⚡
Energy

Community Energy Optimization

Off-grid communities depend on unreliable power that cuts out at the worst moments — when a student needs light to study, when a clinic needs power for refrigeration, when a family needs to cook after dark.

AI-optimized microgrids can distribute solar and wind energy efficiently across a community, predicting usage patterns and balancing supply against demand in real time. Less waste, fewer blackouts, and energy independence for the communities that need it most.

What Happens Next

Each of these five problems will become a GitHub issue on the AI4Change Problems Board. Community members can signal which problems matter most to them. Champions will step forward — people who care enough about a problem to lead the effort of solving it. Teams will form around those champions. And solutions will ship — open source, forever.

This is just the beginning. These five problems are seeds. As the community grows, more problems will be submitted, triaged, and championed. The board is open to anyone. If your community faces a challenge that AI could help solve, submit it.

The problems don't wait for us to be ready. So we start now, with what we have, where we are.
